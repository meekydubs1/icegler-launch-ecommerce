Our story — 64°08′N

From the edge
of the map.

Icegler began in Reykjavík with a simple idea: that the clearest light in the world deserves eyewear made with the same clarity. We design from the cold, for anyone who lives in it.

[ Full-bleed editorial — glacier coast at dusk ]
“In winter the sun barely clears the horizon. The whole island turns the colour of a held breath.”
01 — The light

Iceland gives you two kinds of light, and nothing in between.

There is the long blue dusk of winter, when the day is a rumour and the snow does the work the sun won't. And there is the white, ceaseless light of summer, when the night never fully arrives and the glaciers throw it back at you from every angle.

Both are hard on the eyes. Both are beautiful. We grew up squinting into them, and we built Icegler for exactly that — frames that earn their place on your face when the light is at its most extreme, and its most lovely.

Every decision we make, from the curve of a lens to the weight of an arm, is measured against that standard: would it hold up to an Icelandic afternoon?

The philosophy
We make one promise: nothing hidden. The frame you hold is exactly what it appears to be — clear material, honest craft, no shortcuts frozen inside.
02 — The iceblock

Why we freeze our frames in ice.

It started as a photograph — a pair of frames set inside a clear block of glacier ice, lit from behind. We loved what it said before we could explain it.

Ice hides nothing. Look through it and you see every bubble, every fracture, every honest flaw. We want our frames to be read the same way: clarity you can hold up to the light. The iceblock became our signature, and our standard.

03 — The making

Slow hands, good material.

Nothing here is rushed. A single frame passes through a dozen pairs of hands before it earns the Icegler mark.

01
Plant-based acetate

Our acetate is made from cotton and wood pulp, not oil. It cuts warmer, polishes glassier, and returns to the earth at the end of its long life.

02
Carl Zeiss lenses

Every pair leaves with German Zeiss glass — 100% UV400, polarized, and ground to cut the low, hard glare you only get this far north.

03
Hand-finished in Reykjavík

Tumbled for three days, hand-assembled, and inspected one frame at a time by the small team that designed it. Built to be kept, not replaced.
