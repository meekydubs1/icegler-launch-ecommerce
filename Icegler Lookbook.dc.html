Winter
Light

A season shot in the long blue dusk — the Icegler collection on glacier, basalt and ice. Eight frames, one kind of light.

“We waited for the four o'clock dark, when the sky goes the colour of a bruise and the snow keeps the last of the light.”
Notes from the shoot

Photographed over three days across the south coast — Reykjavík, Vík, and the glacier lagoon at Jökulsárlón. No studio, no sun. Only the flat, generous light that the north gives for a few hours in January.

Styling kept deliberately spare. The frames, the cold, the model, the ice. Nothing else in the frame to argue with.
